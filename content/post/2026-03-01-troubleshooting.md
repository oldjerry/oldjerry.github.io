## Overview

In business environments, system issues can directly affect daily operations, employee productivity, and customer service.

Over the years, I have worked on troubleshooting and recovering a wide range of production issues, including application failures, system instability, network interruptions, and infrastructure-related incidents.

My focus has always been to restore service quickly, identify the actual cause, and reduce the risk of the same issue happening again.

---

## The Challenge

Critical system issues often happen under pressure:

- Business operations are already affected  
- Users need immediate support  
- The root cause is not always obvious  
- Documentation may be incomplete  
- Multiple systems can be involved  

In these situations, reacting too quickly without understanding the issue can create more risk.

## My Approach

I follow a structured troubleshooting and recovery process focused on both speed and reliability.

### 1. Assess Impact and Stabilize

The first step is to quickly understand:

- What systems are affected  
- Who is impacted  
- Whether this is isolated or system-wide  
- The immediate business risk  

Then I focus on stabilizing the environment:

- Restore critical services if possible  
- Apply temporary workarounds if needed  
- Reduce further disruption  

---

### 2. Gather Facts and Verify Symptoms

Before making major changes, I collect useful information:

- User reports and recent changes  
- System logs and alerts  
- Service dependencies  
- Network and server health  

A clear picture of symptoms helps avoid guessing and unnecessary changes. Checking logs and reproducing issues where possible are often the fastest path to useful clues.  [oai_citation:1‡PDQ](https://www.pdq.com/blog/common-problem-solving-techniques-for-sysadmins/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

### 3. Identify Root Cause

Once services are stable, I work through the issue methodically:

- Review recent deployments or configuration changes  
- Check system resources and service health  
- Validate permissions and connectivity  
- Isolate the failure point  

In complex systems, the issue is often not a single failure, but a chain of contributing factors. I focus on identifying the most actionable root cause rather than making assumptions.  [oai_citation:2‡Medium](https://medium.com/%40karthikmani84/it-infrastructure-troubleshooting-framework-539bae5e7c3b?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

### 4. Recover and Validate

After applying a fix, I verify that the issue is fully resolved:

- Confirm services are restored  
- Test business-critical workflows  
- Monitor for recurring symptoms  
- Confirm with users or stakeholders  

Recovery is not complete until the business can operate normally again.

---

### 5. Document and Prevent

A good recovery process should improve future reliability.

I typically document:

- What happened  
- Root cause or likely cause  
- Resolution steps  
- Risks or gaps discovered  
- Recommended improvements  

Strong documentation, inventory, and access reviews are essential to prevent repeat incidents. Experienced IT teams consistently emphasize asset visibility, backup verification, and access control as the foundation for reliable support.
